# Nivaesa Housing LLC — Business Plan

## 1. Executive Summary

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Company Name** | Nivaesa Housing LLC |
| **Business Type** | Housing marketplace (leases, subleases, roommates) for South Asians in the U.S. |
| **Mission** | Make housing discovery safer, faster, and culturally compatible for South Asian renters and landlords |
| **Current Stage** | Prototype/MVP in progress; planning metro-by-metro launch |

### Problem
South Asian renters rely heavily on fragmented WhatsApp/Facebook groups with weak search, high scam risk, and poor matching for lifestyle/cultural requirements.

### Solution
A structured marketplace with map search, cultural preference filters, and trust/verification signals designed specifically for South Asians.

### Target Market
- South Asian students, early-career professionals, newcomers
- Landlords/sublessors/property managers serving SA-dense metros

### Business Model
1. Promoted listings
2. Pay-per-lead
3. Landlord subscriptions
4. Paid verification
5. Escrow/transaction services (later)

### Near-Term Goals (90 days)
Launch MVP in one metro, seed listings, establish community partnerships, validate inquiry-to-tour funnel.

---

## 2. Company Description

### 2.1 Overview
Nivaesa Housing LLC is a marketplace platform that helps South Asians in the U.S. find compatible housing options—leases, subleases, and roommates—using culturally relevant filters and trust-first workflows.

### 2.2 Vision
Become the default housing platform for South Asians nationwide, starting with the highest-density metros and expanding city-by-city.

### 2.3 Objectives
- Build and launch a functional MVP with map-based browsing and high-intent inquiry flows
- Achieve density in one metro (supply + demand) before expanding
- Establish repeatable growth channels through community distribution partnerships
- Introduce monetization after engagement proves healthy marketplace liquidity

---

## 3. Market Opportunity

### 3.1 Customer Segments

**Demand (renters):**
- International and domestic South Asian students
- New professionals (H1B/OPT/relocation)
- Short-term renters (internships, rotations, seasonal stays)

**Supply (inventory providers):**
- South Asian homeowners/landlords
- Existing tenants subleasing rooms
- Property managers and small-scale operators
- Agents (optional channel, not required for marketplace function)

### 3.2 Pain Points
- Low trust and high scam exposure in informal channels
- Cultural/lifestyle mismatches not supported by mainstream housing sites
- Inefficient search/discovery and poor listing quality control
- High coordination friction for tours, approvals, and short-term stays

### 3.3 Beachhead Strategy
Win one metro first by targeting communities with:
- High South Asian density
- High rental turnover
- Active existing housing group activity

---

## 4. Products & Services

### 4.1 Core Product (Marketplace)
- Listings for lease / sublease / roommate
- Map + list browsing with structured filters
- Inquiry workflow (lead tracking + templates)
- Saved searches + alerts (retention)

### 4.2 Cultural Preference Filters (Differentiation)
Examples:
- Vegetarian-only
- Women-only
- Family-friendly
- No alcohol/smoking
- Quiet home
- Prayer-friendly

### 4.3 Trust & Safety Layer
- Email/phone verification (MVP)
- Profile strength indicators
- Reporting/moderation workflow
- Paid identity verification (post-MVP)

### 4.4 Future Services (Later Stage)
- Deposit/escrow options via compliant partners
- Lease support: templates, education, renter protections
- Partnerships: renters insurance, moving services, utilities setup

---

## 5. Competitive Analysis

### 5.1 Alternatives

| Platform | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Zillow / Apartments.com** | Scale and inventory | Weak cultural matching, no sublease trust workflows |
| **Facebook / WhatsApp groups** | Culturally relevant | Unstructured, unverified, scam-prone |
| **Roommate apps** | Generic matching | Limited cultural nuance and community distribution |

### 5.2 Differentiation / Moat
- Cultural-fit discovery built into the product (not "tags slapped on")
- Trust-first sublease workflow and moderation
- Community distribution partnerships that mainstream platforms don't have

## 6. Business Model

### 6.1 Revenue Streams (Sequenced)

**Phase 1 — Marketplace Revenue**
- Promoted listings (boost visibility)
- Pay-per-lead (charged to landlords/agents for qualified inquiries)
- Landlord subscriptions (leads + tools)

**Phase 2 — Trust Revenue**
- Paid verification badge (identity checks)
- Premium renter profile (priority visibility, faster responses)

**Phase 3 — Transaction Revenue (Later)**
- Escrow/deposit handling via compliant partner or licensed framework
- Ancillary partnerships and service marketplace

### 6.2 Pricing (Initial Targets)

| Product | Price |
|---------|-------|
| Promoted listing | $10–$50 (depending on duration/placement) |
| Pay-per-lead | $5–$25 (depending on lead quality/market) |
| Landlord subscription | $49–$199/month (based on volume/tools) |
| Verification | $10–$25 one-time |

## 7. Go-To-Market Strategy

### 7.1 Distribution Channels
- South Asian student associations (SASA), grad orgs, international offices
- Community centers, temples, cultural orgs, cricket clubs
- Partnerships with housing group admins (offer better tooling + verified workflows)

### 7.2 Growth Loops
- "Share to WhatsApp" listing links (native virality)
- Saved searches + alerts (retention)
- Invite/referral incentives (unlock boosts or verification discounts)

### 7.3 Launch Plan (Metro 1)
- Seed 200–500 listings (manual outreach + community partners)
- Weekly "verified drop" posts into community channels
- Tight feedback loop: improve listing quality and inquiry conversion

## 8. Operations Plan

### 8.1 Key Activities

- Community partnerships + inventory acquisition
- Customer support and trust escalation

### 8.2 Team Structure (Early)
- Founder/PM: product, partnerships, growth

## 10. Traction & Key Metrics

|--------|---------|

## 11. Financial Plan

### 11.1 Assumptions (Early Stage)
- City-by-city expansion (avoid thin markets)
- Monetize once marketplace liquidity is visible (avoid paywalls too early)
- Moderate acquisition costs through community distribution

### 11.2 Example Revenue Model (Single Metro)

| Revenue Stream | Calculation | Monthly |
|----------------|-------------|---------|
| Landlord subscriptions | 200 × $49/mo | $9,800 |
| Promoted listings | 1,000 × $10 | $10,000 |
| Verifications | 1,000 × $15 | $15,000 (one-time) |

### 11.3 Major Cost Drivers
- Engineering + hosting (early: low)
- Moderation + support (scales with volume)
- Partnership/community ops (high leverage, low spend)

## 12. Risk Analysis

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|

| Liquidity/chicken-and-egg | Metro focus, seed supply first |
| Regulatory/escrow complexity | Delay to later phase; partner for compliance |
